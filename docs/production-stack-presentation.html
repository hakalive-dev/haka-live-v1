Haka Live's production infrastructure is designed to support a live social audio and video platform from early launch through significant scale. Every service has been selected to balance cost predictability, operational simplicity, and growth readiness.

The stack is hosted on Render (backend compute), Supabase (database, authentication, and media storage), and Cloudflare (global CDN and DDoS protection). Real-time audio and video rooms are powered by Agora — the same engine used by BIGO Live, Clubhouse, and Yalla.

At early stage, total infrastructure spend begins at approximately $205/month for under 100 concurrent users. As the platform grows, the primary variable is Agora's usage-based audio billing — a cost that scales in direct proportion to user engagement and, therefore, revenue opportunity.

Key Design Principle
All services are production-grade managed platforms — no self-hosted infrastructure to maintain. The stack is optimised for a lean engineering team, with authentication, storage, caching, and monitoring all handled by specialist providers.

Cost Spotlight: Agora
Variable Cost Deep-Dive

Agora is the real-time audio and video engine powering Haka Live's live rooms — the same technology used by BIGO Live, Clubhouse, and Yalla. It is billed per participant-minute, meaning costs grow only when users are actively engaged on the platform.

Why This Is an Opportunity, Not a Risk
Agora costs rise only when users are active in rooms. Active users are the same users sending virtual gifts and purchasing coins. Every increase in Agora spend is accompanied by a proportional increase in platform revenue.

Why These Choices
Strategic Rationale
1
Supabase over separate database, auth, and storage providers
Supabase consolidates PostgreSQL, user authentication, and media file storage into a single $25/month Pro plan. The alternative — a standalone managed database ($50–80/mo), a separate auth service, and an S3-equivalent for storage — would cost $90–130/month with three separate vendor relationships to manage. Supabase also includes daily automated backups, connection pooling, and a built-in admin interface.
2
Firebase for Phone OTP, Supabase Auth for session management
Firebase Auth delivers phone OTP without requiring a third-party SMS provider — the same service used by WhatsApp and BIGO Live for phone verification. Supabase Auth then manages all user sessions and access tokens from that point forward. Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) handles push notifications to both iOS and Android at no cost, up to 1 million messages per day.
3
Render over self-managed cloud infrastructure
Render provides managed Node.js hosting with zero DevOps overhead — no Kubernetes clusters, no load balancer configuration, no EC2 management. For an early-stage platform, this translates directly to engineering hours saved and faster product iteration. The backend is fully containerised with Docker, so migrating to AWS, GCP, or Fly.io at higher scale is straightforward when the time comes.

Payment Gateway
To be Discussed
Decision Pending
The payment gateway provider and commercial model are yet to be finalised. This section will be updated once a decision has been reached by the founding team.

Key considerations for the payment gateway selection:

  • Supported payment methods — card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, regional wallets, and alternative rails
  • Target market coverage — transaction fees and availability vary significantly by region; key markets must be confirmed first
  • Fee model — percentage per transaction versus flat monthly fee impacts unit economics at scale
  • Payout and withdrawal support — agents and hosts require reliable earnings withdrawal to bank accounts or wallets
  • Chargeback and fraud handling — virtual goods and coin purchases are a high-risk category; provider must support chargeback mitigation tools
  • Crypto and alternative payment rails — relevant for markets with limited card penetration or where users prefer non-card methods
